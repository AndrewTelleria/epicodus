About

Hello friend, I am Andrew K. Telleria and I am a software developer. To give you a little background on myself I am a Oregon native and I grew up in Salem, Oregon. I am a big fan of the outdoors and really enjoy going out in the middle of no where - it gives me perspective on issues I think are unique to me when they are not. I have just picked up a new hobby - wood working.

I started programming Janurary 2016 at Epicodus in Portland, Oregon. It was a coding bootcamp that lasted until the end of May of 2016 and it was forty hours a week. It really did immerse me into the world of programming. After I graduated from Epicodus I still did not feel job ready and some concepts still did not sink in all the way.

Being that I did not feel ready I picked up a job for the time being at a restaurant and started to teach myself Python. Ever since I started learning Python all the concepts from Epicodus started to make sense. I would say this was my "Ah-ha!" moment. I completed the Treehouse courses on Python basics and the intro to Flask course. Once I felt confident enough I went through the Flask Mega Tutorial and built a blog from the ground, up. Here is my gitHub repo with the blog.

Now I am enrolled into Hack Oregon Python II course. I am going to write posts on what we are learning to document for myself and whoever is interested in the same topics.
